The Doors of Durin

Speak, friend, and enter.

The Enchanted Gateway to Moria

The Doors of Durin, also known as the West-gate of Moria, are not just a doorway but a symbol of ancient friendship and mystery.

Carved into the side of the Misty Mountains, these doors served as a passage between the Dwarves of Khazad-dum and the Elves of Eregion.

Invisible to the eye unless touched by the light of the moon or stars, these gates guard one of Middle-earth's greatest secrets.

History

The doors were constructed during the Second Age by the Dwarves of Khazad-dum in collaboration with the Elves of Eregion.The doors were intended to serve as a passage between the two allied realms.

The doors are unique in that they were made of ithildin, a substance that could only be seen by starlight or moonlight, and were inscribed with Elvish runes that spelled out the famous riddle: "Speak, friend, and enter."

Secrets of the Doors

Ithildin Glow

Made from mithril, ithildin is a magical substance that only reveals itself in the light of the moon and stars. It reflects the ancient bond between Dwarves and Elves

Elvish Inscription

The inscription reads "Ennyn Durin Aran Moria: Pedo mellon a minno" which translates to "The Doors of Durin, Lord of Moria. Speak, friend, and enter

Enigmatic Mechanism

Unlike conventional doors, the Doors of Durin can only be opened by speaking the Elvish word for "friend"-a riddle that has puzzled many over the ages.

Legend and Lore

The Doors of Durin have storied history in Middle-earth, but they are perhaps best known for their role in *The Lord of the Rings*. When the Fellowship of the Ring arrived at the West-gate, Gandalf was initially stumped by the riddle inscripted on the door.

It was only after much contemplation that he realized the simplicity of the solution: the Elvish word "mellon" (friend) was the key. The moment the word was spoken, the doors opened, revealed the dark passage into Moria.

Establishment of Eregion and the forging of the Rings of Power The whereabouts of Celebrimbor during the early years of the Second Age are uncertain, but at some point, he became a great friend to Galadriel and Celeborn,[1] and in S.A. 750,[9] Celebrimbor with many others of the exiled Noldor followed them to establish the realm of Eregion near to Khazad-dûm, the mansions of the Longbeard Dwarves. This they did due to the discovery of mithril in the surrounding mountains.[10] Eventually, a great friendship arose between the Elves of Eregion and the Dwarves of Khazad-dûm, such that was never before seen, and Celebrimbor became the Lord of Eregion[10] and the greatest of the Gwaith-i-Mírdain, "People of the Jewel-smiths", who in their skill were second only to Fëanor, Celebrimbor's grandfather. There, Celebrimbor befriended Narvi the Dwarf,[11] who built the Doors of Durin, and Celebrimbor inscribed the words upon it.[12] However, in S.A. 1200, Sauron, under the fair guise of Annatar, came to Eriador posing as an emissary of the Valar. He was rejected by Gil-galad, the High King of the Noldor, in Lindon (where he was not even admitted to), but although Galadriel did not trust him and treated him with scorn, he was nonetheless accepted by the smiths of Eregion, for Celebrimbor desired in his heart to rival the skill and fame of Fëanor. Galadriel and Celeborn were eventually stripped of their authority in Eregion, and this was largely due to the machinations of Sauron. Galadriel, with her daughter Celebrían, departed for the Elvish realm of Lórien, while Celeborn, due to his dislike of all Dwarves and not wanting to cross through their mansions in Khazad-dûm, remained behind in Eregion, and he was largely ignored by Celebrimbor and Annatar thereafter.[11]
