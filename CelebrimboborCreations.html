The Doors of Durin

Speak, friend, and enter.

Introduction

The Doors of Durin, also known as the West-gate or the West-door of Moria, are an iconic enterance to the ancient Dwarven kingdom of Khazad-dum (later known as Moria) in J.R.R Tolkien's Middle-earth.

History

The doors were constructed during the Second Age by the Dwarves of Khazad-dum in collaboration with the Elves of Eregion.The doors were intended to serve as a passage between the two allied realms.

The doors are unique in that they were made of ithildin, a substance that could only be seen by starlight or moonlight, and were inscribed with Elvish runes that spelled out the famous riddle: "Speak, friend, and enter."

Features of the Doors

  • MaterialIthildin, a magical substance that glows only in the light of the moon and stars
  • InscriptionThe inscription reads "Ennyn Durin Aran Moria: Pedo mellon a minno," which translates to "The Doors of Durin, Lord of Moria. Speak, friend, and enter."
  • MechanismThe doors could only be opened by speaking the Elvish word for "friend" ("mellon").

In the Lore

The Doors of Durin played a significant role in the journey of the Fellowship of the Ring. Gandalf initially struggled to open the doors, but eventually realized that the word "mellon" was the key to entry
